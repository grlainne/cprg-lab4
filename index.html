What's a vinyl record?

Vinyl records were first introduced in the late 19th century but gained significant popularity in the mid-20th century, particularly from the 1950s through the 1980s. They come in various sizes, with the most common being 12 inches in diameter for LPs (long-playing records) and 7 inches for singles.

Vinyl records have grooves engraved on both sides that contain analog sound information. A stylus, or needle, is used to track these grooves, converting the physical movements into electrical signals that can be amplified and played through speakers.
